Our Work

Save the Children fights for the right of children to have a future.

In Save the Children we believe that all children and adolescents deserve to have a decent present and future. In South Africa and around the world, we work every day to give children a good start in life, as well as the opportunity to learn and be protected from violence or any other damage.

We make sure that they meet their specific needs and that their voices are heard. We achieve lasting results for millions of children and adolescents, including those who are more difficult to reach. Global Strategy: Ambition for Children

In 2015, the Save the Children movement agreed a new global strategy - Ambition for Children 2030. We also published a strategic plan for 2016-2018. Through this strategy, we will harness our resources, energy, knowledge and expertise to drive our three global breakthroughs for 2030:


Survive

NO child dies from preventable causes before their fifth birthday

LEARN

all children learn from a good quality basic education

BE PROTECTED

violence against children is no longer tolerated

To achieve lasting and sustainable changes in the lives of children and adolescents, it is necessary to push governments, civil society, academia, the private sector, the media, and children and adolescents collectively, to formulate laws, public policies, programs and public budgets that promote and ethically respect their rights, particularly those most excluded.

Our Advocacy and Governance work is based on:
  • Sustainable development agenda
  • Every Last Girl
  • Protection System
  • Migration
  • Child Survival
  • G20
  • Vulnerability of Girls and Adolescents
  • Investment in Childhood
  • World Breastfeeding Week
In South Africa and around the world, we make sure that children affected by floods, hurricanes, fires, famines, earthquakes, epidemics and armed conflicts are protected and receive medical help, shelters, food, water, etc.

We protect them and help them to reunite with their families. We help them recover from crises through emotional support and safe spaces to learn and play.We also work from a preventive approach to reduce disaster risks in certain communities.
